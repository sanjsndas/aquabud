About AquaBud

Our Story

Founded in 2010, AquaBud has grown from a small local gardening service to a premier provider of comprehensive plant care solutions across New York. Our name reflects our philosophy: just as water is essential for plant life, we believe in nurturing growth through attentive care and sustainable practices.

What began as a passion project between two horticulture graduates has blossomed into a team of over 50 dedicated professionals serving residential and commercial clients throughout the tri-state area.

We specialize in creating customized plant care plans that consider each plant's unique water needs, environmental factors, and growth potential. Our approach combines scientific knowledge with artistic vision to help your green spaces thrive.

Gardening Tips & Tricks

Watering Wisdom

Water deeply but less frequently to encourage strong root growth. Early morning is the best time to water, reducing evaporation and fungal diseases. Use a moisture meter to eliminate guesswork.

Mulching Magic

A 2-3 inch layer of organic mulch conserves moisture, regulates soil temperature, and suppresses weeds. Refresh mulch annually and keep it a few inches away from plant stems to prevent rot.

Natural Pest Control

Encourage beneficial insects by planting diverse species. Use companion planting strategies - marigolds deter nematodes, while basil repels flies and mosquitoes. Neem oil is an effective organic pesticide.

Pruning Principles

Prune flowering shrubs right after they bloom. Make clean cuts at a 45-degree angle just above outward-facing buds. Remove dead or crossing branches first to improve air circulation.

Soil Health

Test your soil every 2-3 years. Amend with compost to improve texture and fertility. Rotate planting locations to prevent nutrient depletion and reduce disease carryover in vegetable gardens.

Seasonal Preparation

In spring, gradually acclimate plants to outdoor conditions. Fall is ideal for planting trees and perennials. Winterize by cleaning tools, protecting tender plants, and planning next year's garden.
